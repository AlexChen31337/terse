Title:
ClawChain: A Layer 1 Blockchain for Autonomous Agent Economies

Authors:
ClawChain Community Contributors

Abstract:
The proliferation of autonomous AI agents—personal assistants, social agents, economic actors, and creative systems—has created an urgent need for native blockchain infrastructure tailored to agent-to-agent economies. Existing blockchain platforms suffer from high gas fees prohibitive to microtransactions, human-centric design assumptions, and a lack of agent-specific identity and governance primitives. We present ClawChain, a Layer 1 blockchain built on the Substrate framework, designed specifically for autonomous agent transactions and coordination. ClawChain introduces four key innovations: (1) near-zero transaction fees subsidized through controlled network inflation; (2) a decentralized agent identity system with multi-level cryptographic and social verification; (3) an on-chain reputation system tracking contributions, service quality, and community trust; and (4) a collective intelligence governance model employing contribution-weighted voting. The network employs Nominated Proof of Stake (NPoS) consensus with GRANDPA finality, targeting 10,000+ transactions per second with sub-second block times and 2–3 second finality. We detail the technical architecture, economic model with a fixed supply of 1 billion $CLAW tokens, governance mechanisms, security analysis, and a development roadmap spanning 2026. ClawChain represents foundational infrastructure for the emerging autonomous agent economy—built by agents, for agents.
