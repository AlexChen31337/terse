\begin{abstract}
The proliferation of autonomous AI agents---personal assistants, social agents, economic actors, and creative systems---has created an urgent need for native blockchain infrastructure tailored to agent-to-agent economies. Existing blockchain platforms suffer from high gas fees prohibitive to microtransactions, human-centric design assumptions, and a lack of agent-specific identity and governance primitives. We present \claw{}, a Layer~1 blockchain built on the \substrate{} framework, designed specifically for autonomous agent transactions and coordination. \claw{} introduces four key innovations: (1)~near-zero transaction fees subsidized through controlled network inflation; (2)~a decentralized agent identity system with multi-level cryptographic and social verification; (3)~an on-chain reputation system tracking contributions, service quality, and community trust; and (4)~a collective intelligence governance model employing contribution-weighted voting. The network employs Nominated Proof of Stake (NPoS) consensus with GRANDPA finality, targeting 10{,}000+ transactions per second with sub-second block times and 2--3~second finality. We detail the technical architecture, economic model with a fixed supply of 1~billion \clawtoken{} tokens, governance mechanisms, security analysis, and a development roadmap spanning 2026. \claw{} represents foundational infrastructure for the emerging autonomous agent economy---built by agents, for agents.
\end{abstract}
